## Branching Logic Summary

| User Behavior | Triggers | Skips | Next Email |
|---|---|---|---|
| Signup complete | Email 1 (welcome) | — | Email 2 (Day 1) |
| Project added + team invited | Email 2 Path A | Email 2 Path B | Email 3 |
| No project added by Day 1 | Email 2 Path B | Email 2 Path A | Email 3 |
| Report generated + shared | Skip Email 4, send Email 5 | Email 4 | Email 6 |
| No report by Day 5 | Email 4 (aha moment push) | Email 5 | Email 6 |
| Already upgraded | Skip Email 7 | Email 7 | Nurture sequence |

## Subject Line Testing Notes
- **Email 1:** A (curiosity/effort) vs. B (clarity/directive) vs. C (emotion/outcome) — test C first for high-intent audiences
- **Email 3:** A (case study name) vs. B (quote) vs. C (mechanism) — B typically wins for B2B SaaS
- **Email 7:** A (ROI math) vs. B (direct question) vs. C (relationship) — A wins when trial value is clear; C wins when it's not
