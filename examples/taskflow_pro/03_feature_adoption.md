# Example Output — Prompt 3: Feature Adoption Campaign
## Product: TaskFlow Pro | Feature: Client Portal

**Feature:** Client Portal — controlled external access to live project dashboards and status reports
**Why users don't adopt it:** They set up the report, but sharing with a client feels risky — they're not sure what the client will see, whether internal notes are exposed, or whether the portal looks professional enough
**Power user:** Agency account manager, 10-50 person firm, managing 3+ active client projects
**Result:** Client email inquiries about project status drop 80%; account managers recover 2-3 hours/week

---

## Email 1 — The Problem

**Send timing:** Day 21 (or 7 days after user reaches aha moment, whichever comes first)
**Trigger condition:** User has generated at least one report but has NOT activated client portal access

**Subject line A:** Why clients keep emailing you even after you send the update
**Subject line B:** The "one more question" email problem
**Subject line C:** You answered their question. They had three more.
**Preview text:** Every update you send creates two more questions

**Body:**
Hey [First Name],

You send a project update. Your client reads it. Then they email back:

"Quick question — is the logo design included in that milestone?"
"Who's the main contact if I need to escalate something?"
"Can you send me last month's update too? I want to compare."

You've done the work of writing the update. Now you're doing more work answering questions about the update.

The pattern is predictable: every summary you send becomes a conversation. Not because your clients are difficult — because static updates create questions that real-time visibility would answer automatically.

There's a way to fix this that doesn't involve writing more detailed updates.

More on that Thursday.

– Marcus
